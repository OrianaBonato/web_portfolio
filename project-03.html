Gocho

For GOCHO, I wanted to build a logotype with character — something bold that would make an impression right away. That's why we went with a chunky condensed typeface, and that's where my favorite detail of the whole project came from: compressing the letter H so the brand itself gets "smashed," a direct nod to the smash technique. It's the kind of detail that, once you notice it, stays with you.

With color, I wanted to tell the same story: black represents the grill, giving it that grittier, gastrobar edge, while orange brings in the warmth of toasted bread and melted cheese. A small palette, but built to work just as well on a tiny label as on a large-format vinyl.

What I love most about this project is how well the system holds up across every touchpoint: packaging, t-shirts, social media, even the food truck. We added sticker-style illustrations — the mascot character, the sauce splatters — to bring in that playful, approachable feel without losing the strength of the core brand. The result is an identity built to grow alongside the business, ready to scale into whatever format it needs.
